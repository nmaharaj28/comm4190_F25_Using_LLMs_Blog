Can AI cure bordem

I was bored, and struggling to think of a blog post for this week. So, naturally, I told anthropic/claude-3.5-sonnet that I was bored. This is how the conversation went:

USER

I’m bored

ASSISTANT

Here are some suggestions to help with boredom:

  1. Learn something new:
  • Try a new language on Duolingo
  • Watch educational YouTube videos
  • Take an online course
  1. Get creative:
  • Draw or color
  • Write a story
  • Start a journal
  • Make music
  1. Be active:
  • Do a home workout
  • Dance to your favorite music
  • Practice yoga
  • Go for a walk
  1. Entertainment:
  • Read a book
  • Start a new TV series
  • Play video games
  • Do puzzles or brain teasers
  1. Productive activities:
  • Organize your space
  • Try a new recipe
  • Start a DIY project
  • Make a bucket list
  1. Social activities:
  • Call a friend or family member
  • Join an online community
  • Play online multiplayer games
  • Start a virtual book club

What interests you the most from these suggestions? I can provide more specific ideas based on your preferences!

USER

Play an online game with me

ASSISTANT

I can’t actually play online games directly with you, as I’m an AI language model and don’t have the capability to interact in real-time gaming environments. However, I can:

  1. Play simple text-based games with you, like:
  • Word Association
  • 20 Questions
  • Riddles
  • Hangman
  • Number Guessing
  • Trivia
  1. Or I can recommend some online games you can play:
  • With friends: Among Us, Minecraft, Words With Friends
  • Browser games: Agar.io, Slither.io
  • Mobile games: Pokemon GO, Candy Crush
  • Online multiplayer: Fortnite, Roblox

Would you like to try one of the text-based games with me, or would you prefer recommendations for online games you can play with others?

USER

Let’s play 20 questions

ASSISTANT

Great choice! I’ll think of something (it can be a person, place, thing, or animal), and you try to guess what it is by asking me up to 20 yes/no questions.

I’ve got something in mind! Remember, you can only ask questions that I can answer with “yes” or “no.”

Go ahead and ask your first question!

USER

Are you an inanimate object?

ASSISTANT

Yes, I am an inanimate object!

19 questions remaining. What’s your next question?

USER

Are you a household appliance?

ASSISTANT

No, I am not a household appliance.

18 questions remaining! Keep going!

USER

Are you something found in an office?

ASSISTANT

Yes, I am something commonly found in an office!

17 questions remaining. You’re making good progress!

USER

Can you be found on a desk?

ASSISTANT

Yes, I can be found on a desk!

16 questions remaining. You’re getting warmer!

USER

Are you used for writing or typing?

ASSISTANT

No, I’m not used for writing or typing.

15 questions remaining! Think about other common desk items…

USER

Are you used to measure?

ASSISTANT

Yes, I am used to measure!

14 questions remaining. You’re getting very close now!

USER

Are you a ruler or measuring tape?

ASSISTANT

Yes! I am a ruler! You got it!

Well done! It took you only 7 questions to figure it out. You used a great strategy by narrowing it down from inanimate object → office item → desk item → measuring tool → ruler.

Would you like to play another round? This time you can think of something and I’ll try to guess it!

Reflection

This was interesting! The AI was able to give suggestions to cure my bordem, which ultimately led to us playing 20 question. It was interesting to see how the AI maintained clear rules and kept track of my remaining questions, which helped me stay organized. I noticed how it gave encouraging responses after each of my questions, using phrases like “you’re getting warmer!” and “you’re making good progress!” which made AI feel more human. It didn’t just give yes/no answers but kept the conversation flowing naturally. I also appreciated how it recapped my strategy at the end, showing how I narrowed down the possibilities step by step.
