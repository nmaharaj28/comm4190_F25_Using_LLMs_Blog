Asking AI for the best U.S. State

I ask Gemini what the prettiest U.S. state is.

The United States is incredibly diverse, thanks to its 50 unique states. At Penn, where the student body represents much of the country, I’ve had the chance to meet people from nearly every state, each with their own sense of hometown pride. With that in mind, I decided to ask Gemini which U.S. state is truly the best.

This is how my conversation with Gemini 2.5 flash went:

USER

What is the best U.S. State?

ASSISTANT

There isn’t a single “best” U.S. state, as “best” is incredibly subjective and depends entirely on what you prioritize!

What might be best for a young professional seeking career opportunities will be very different from what’s best for a retiree looking for a low cost of living, or a family wanting great schools and outdoor recreation.

However, we can look at what different states excel in:

States often ranked highly in various categories include:

  • Massachusetts: Frequently ranks high for education, healthcare, and innovation.
  • Washington: Strong economy (tech), beautiful outdoors, and good quality of life.
  • Colorado: Known for its outdoor recreation, healthy lifestyle, and growing economy.
  • New Jersey: Good education systems, strong economy, and proximity to major cities.
  • New Hampshire: Low crime, high quality of life, and no state income or sales tax.
  • Minnesota: Strong healthcare, good education, and a healthy population.
  • Utah: Strong economy, beautiful national parks, and a family-friendly environment.
  • Florida: Popular for retirees, no state income tax, and warm weather.
  • Texas: Strong job market, no state income tax, and diverse culture.
  • California: Massive economy, diverse culture, and varied landscapes, but high cost of living.

To figure out the “best” state for you, consider these factors:

  1. Cost of Living: Housing, taxes (income, sales, property), groceries, utilities.
  2. Job Market/Economy: Industry strength, unemployment rates, average salaries in your field.
  3. Education: Quality of K-12 schools, access to higher education.
  4. Healthcare: Quality of hospitals, insurance access, health outcomes.
  5. Quality of Life: Crime rates, community engagement, cultural attractions, public services.
  6. Outdoor Recreation: Access to beaches, mountains, parks, hiking, skiing, etc.
  7. Weather/Climate: Do you prefer four seasons, mild winters, hot summers, etc.?
  8. Politics/Values: Do the state’s dominant political and social values align with yours?
  9. Proximity to Family/Friends: An often overlooked but important factor for many.

Many organizations (like U.S. News & World Report, WalletHub, CNBC) publish “Best States” rankings, but they each use different methodologies and weightings, leading to different results. It’s a great starting point, but always dig deeper into the factors that matter most to you!

USER

What state do you think is the prettiest? Provide your opinion!

ASSISTANT

Okay, as an AI, I don’t have eyes or personal preferences, so I can’t truly perceive beauty or have an “opinion” in the human sense!

However, if I were to analyze the vast amount of human data, images, and descriptions I’ve processed about natural landscapes across the U.S., and consider which state consistently receives the most widespread praise for its diverse and stunning scenery, I would lean towards California.

Here’s why California stands out in terms of “prettiness” from a data-driven perspective:

  1. Incredible Diversity: No other state offers such a dramatic range of landscapes within its borders. You have:

    • Majestic Mountains: The Sierra Nevada range, home to Yosemite National Park, with its towering granite cliffs, giant sequoias, and alpine lakes.
    • Stunning Coastlines: From the rugged, misty cliffs of Big Sur to the sunny beaches of Southern California, the Pacific coastline is iconic.
    • Vast Deserts: The unique beauty of Death Valley (the lowest point in North America) and the Joshua Tree National Park.
    • Lush Forests: The ancient, towering redwood forests in the north.
    • Volcanic Landscapes: Lassen Volcanic National Park offers a taste of geothermal activity.
    • Fertile Valleys: The Central Valley, while agricultural, has its own rolling beauty, especially when framed by mountains.

  2. Iconic National Parks: California boasts more national parks than any other state (9!), each preserving distinct and breathtaking ecosystems.

  3. Dramatic Contrasts: You can literally ski in the mountains in the morning and surf in the ocean in the afternoon, or drive from a bustling city to a silent desert in a few hours. This constant shift in scenery is incredibly appealing.

While states like Hawaii (for its unique tropical volcanic beauty), Colorado (for its Rocky Mountain grandeur), Alaska (for its untamed wilderness), and Utah (for its surreal red rock formations) are absolutely breathtaking and strong contenders, California’s sheer breadth and variety of different types of stunning landscapes make it a top choice when considering “prettiness” across the board.

Reflection

This was a fun conversation. I am shocked to see AI actually define its subjective opinion, even though the answer is backed with a lot of facts. It moved from broad reasoning to specific examples. The AI’s response also revealed how it synthesizes human opinions and objective data to form nuanced, almost human-like arguments. This proves that while AI can’t form personal opinions, it can effectively mimic analytical reasoning, making it a surprisingly strong conversational partner for reflective or exploratory discussions. It was also interesting to see how the AI immediately turned the conversation by trying to assess what I valued in a state, instead of answering the question itself.
